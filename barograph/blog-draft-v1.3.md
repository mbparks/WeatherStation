# Two Pens

*A short follow-up draft for Gears of Resistance / mbparks.com*

BAROGRAPH v1.3.0 undoes its own sprawl. The wall of registers I built in v1.2 was handsome, but it made you scroll to read the sky. Now there is one drum again, and it does everything.

Beneath the drum sits a row of brass stops, one for each measurement the rooftop reports: pressure, temperature, wind, humidity, rain, sun, ultraviolet. Each stop wears a dot of its own ink. Press two and their pens ride the drum together, the first against the left-hand scale and grid, the newest against the right, with the scale numerals printed in each trace's ink so there is never a question of which numbers belong to which line. Press a third and the oldest pen lifts. The drum insists on keeping at least one; an instrument that records nothing is just furniture.

Every ink kept its character in the move. Wind still bristles with gust flicks. Rain still climbs through the day and siphons to nought at midnight. The sun still draws its bells in burnt umber and lies flat all night. Ultraviolet, newly promoted to a pen of its own, writes in iron gall. Your choice of pens persists between visits, like a good instrument that stays as you left it.

The satisfying part is the code got smaller. Seven measurements on one drum replaced five separate instruments, and the generic strip renderer I was proud of three versions ago is gone entirely. Sometimes the rewrite rule cuts toward less.
