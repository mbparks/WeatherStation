ABOUT THIS INSTRUMENT

BAROGRAPH is Field Instrument No. 067: a Victorian observatory drum that records live weather from a personal weather station. The chart carries seven days of record on aged paper, with a day of blank ruled chart ahead of the present. Rest your pointer anywhere on it and a hairline reads out the values beneath, each in its own ink. Two pens ride the drum at once. The brass stops beneath it choose which measurements they record; the first choice takes the left-hand scale, the newest takes the right, and each writes in its own ink.

Data arrives by way of WX-RELAY, a Cloudflare Worker that keeps the Weather Underground key server-side and caches at the edge. If the wire goes quiet, the instrument falls back to its last cached readings, and failing that, to a demonstration week of synthetic weather.

Local-first: settings and the latest readings live in your browser. Export carries them out; import carries them back in. Append ?test=1 to the address to run the built-in test harness.
